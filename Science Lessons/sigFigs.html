Significant Figures

The rules for determining significant figures are as follows:

  1. All non-zero numbers ARE significant.
  2. Zeros between two non-zero digits ARE significant.
    8007 has FOUR significant figures.
  3. Leading zeros are NOT significant.
    They're nothing more than "place holders." The numbers 0.72 and 0.0084 both have TWO significant figures.
  4. Trailing zeros to the right of the decimal ARE significant.
    1.000 has FOUR significant figures.
  5. Trailing zeros in a whole number with the decimal shown ARE significant.
    200. has THREE significant figures.
  6. Trailing zeros in a whole number with no decimal shown are NOT significant.
    200 has ONE significant figure.

Instructions

Try to determine the number of signifant figures on your own first, then click Sig Figs to check your work!
