No Cap Match Play

The No Cap Matchplay league is a new pilot project this season that is designed to let players play singles match play against others close to their own handicap index thus eliminating the need for strokes. For more information about next year's version contact Ted at ted.shapera@gmail.com.

General News & Wrap Up (October 13)

With only one more week of golf at Larter’s it's time to look back on what worked with the league and what can be improved for next season.

Most of the feedback regarding the league has been positive. Our biggest issue was running out of golf season and getting all of the matches in. Next year we’ll start earlier to avoid this problem.

I will look into whether it would be possible to have a set day for league play such as Mondays (no other leagues other than Senior Interclub in the mornings and less tournaments), which would help alleviate the problem of scheduling matches to some degree.

If you enjoyed playing in the league and know of others who might be interested please ask them to email me (ted.shapera@gmail.com) and I’ll add them to the mailing list for next season.

Blue-White Division

Time running out was definitely an issue as 14 out of 30 matches remained to be played. Cole was leading with 10.5 points and one match remaining, Josh was in second with two matches left, and Chris was in third place with three matches left to play. It would have been a good finish if only we had a few more weeks to play.

Eggshell Division

Congrats to all players in this division as all matches were completed! Aaron finished first with 10 points, and Justin had a strong finish earning 6 points in his last two matches to finish in second with 7.5 points, and Dan was third with 4.5 points
